Every day is Earth Day

Protecting Our Planet Starts with You
Learn how you can help

To help save the environment, try decreasing energy and water consumption; changing your eating and transportation habits to conserve natural resources; and reducing, reusing, and recycling to be more environmentally friendly.

Ten Simple Things You Can Do to Help Protect the Earth

  • 1/10

    Reduce. Reuse. Recycle.

    Cut down on what you throw away. Follow the three “R’s” to concerve natural resources and landfill space.

  • 2/10

    Choose Sustainable Foods

    Sustainable agriculture enables us to produce healthful food without compromising future generations' ability to do the same. Sustainable eating is about choosing foods that are healthful to our environment and our bodies.

  • 3/10

    Shop Wisely

    Buy less plastic and bring a reusable shopping bags. Instead of buying disposable products, which are wasteful, buy things that will last a long time, such as rechargeable batteries and reusable plastic mugs for drinks.

  • 4/10

    Use energy efficient light bulbs

    Energy efficient light bulbs reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Also flip the light switch off when you leave the room.

  • 5/10

    Plant trees

    Threes provide food and oxygen. They help save energy, clean the air, and help combat climate change.

  • 6/10

    Conserve water

    The less water you use, the less runoff and wastewater that eventualy end in the ocean. On top of this, using less warm water makes it possible to reduce greenhouse gas emissions because it avoids the need to generate energy to heat the water.

  • 7/10

    Don’t send chemicals into our waterways

    Pollution in our waterways is harming people and animals. Choose non-toxic chemicals in the home and work.

  • 8/10

    Encourage the use of renewable energy

    Benefits: Generating energy that produces no greenhouse gas emissions from fossil fuels and reduces some types of air pollution. Diversifying energy supply and reducing dependence on imported fuels.

  • 9/10

    Educate Yourself

    When you futher your own education, you can help others understand the importance and value of our natural resources.

  • 10/10

    Volunteer in your community

    Volunteer for cleanups in your community. You can get involved in protecting your watershed too.
